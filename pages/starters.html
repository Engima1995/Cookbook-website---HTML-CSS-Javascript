Canal Floddies

This is a slightly updated version of a dish from the Tyneside town of Gateshead. Traditionally these were made by the men digging the Manchester Ship Canal and fried on their shovels over a fire. Our updated version includes lovage, an old English herb, with a flavour similar to celery. The addition of sun dried tomatoes instead of veggie bacon, give this dish a sweet twist.
